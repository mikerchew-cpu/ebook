The Soul of the Dish: Eggs

Why only yolks matter. Velvety creaminess is strictly achieved through the natural emulsion of yolk, hot starch water, and rendered guanciale fat. The lecithin in yolks is the magic.

The problem with cream. Cream dilutes the deep umami of guanciale and prevents proper starch binding. It masks, rather than reveals, what good ingredients already are.

“ When the egg hits the warm pasta, it doesn't cook — it melts. That's the difference. ”
