Master Class Troubleshooting

When things go wrong — and how to bring them back.

The Symptom

Scrambled Eggs?

The pan was too hot. Residual heat cooked the yolks into curds before they could emulsify.

The FixLet the pan sit longer next run, or splash a tablespoon of cool pasta water before adding the egg paste.
The Symptom

Dry or Sticky Sauce?

The emulsion lost moisture — too much fat absorbed by the pasta, or the pan was too cold.

The FixSlowly stir in reserved pasta starch water, one tablespoon at a time, off the heat, until silken.
The Symptom

Clumping Cheese?

The Pecorino was grated too coarsely, or it was tossed onto dry pasta before the fat was added.

The FixUse a microplane, and always mix the cheese with the yolks into a paste first.
